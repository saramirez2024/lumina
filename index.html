For skin science nerds: The deeper story

GHK-Cu & Collagen Pathways

In cell and animal models, GHK-Cu encourages fibroblasts to make more collagen, elastin and glycosaminoglycans (the "scaffolding" that keeps skin bouncy). It helps balance enzymes that break down old collagen (MMPs) vs. those that build new matrix. In some models, it even nudges growth-factor-related pathways such as TGF-Beta/Smad toward less scarring and more organized tissue.

Most of these studies use isolated cells or animal models. We treat them as supporting evidence for using GHK-Cu in skincare - not as a promise that one serum can rewrite your biology.

What We Know vs. Don't Know Yet

We know: GHK-Cu can stimulate collagen and ECM components in fibroblast cultures. GHK-Cu creams have improved skin appearance (firmness, wrinkles, texture) in small human studies.

We don't fully know: The perfect dose, schedule, or delivery system for every skin type. Exactly how much of the lab magic translates 1:1 to real-world cosmetic doses.

Our stance: We use peer-reviewed data to set our target ranges and then test our actual formulas for stability, safety, and user tolerance. We'll publish key Batch 001 data at launch.

What is GHK-Cu?

GHK-Cu (Copper Tripeptide-1)
Your body already makes this small peptide and pairs it with copper. Levels drop with age. In skin research, GHK-Cu has been shown to support collagen and elastin production and to improve the look of texture, firmness, and fine lines.

We treat it as a gentle "repair signal" - not magic, but a smart nudge in the right direction.

What is Matrixyl 3000?

Matrixyl 3000 (Daily Serum only)
Matrixyl 3000 is a blend of two peptides (palmitoyl tripeptide-1 and palmitoyl tetrapeptide-7) that signal skin to support collagen and elasticity. It's been used for years in anti-aging formulas aimed at firmness and wrinkle appearance.

We use it at 3% in the Daily Remodeling Serum for long-term, low-noise support.

It is not included in the Recovery Vial, which is kept as simple and gentle as possible for disrupted skin.

What is Niacinamide?

Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)
A well-studied ingredient used at 2-5% in clinical moisturizers for barrier function, tone improvement, and inflammation reduction.

We use it at 3% in the Daily Serum to support a stronger, more resilient barrier while the peptides work on texture and firmness.

Not included in Recovery Vials - we keep those formulas minimal for stressed skin.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is LUMINA in one sentence?

LUMINA is a two-product copper peptide skincare system - a Recovery Vial for stressed or post-treatment skin, and a Daily Serum for long-term remodeling - with clear guidance on when to use each.

What's the difference between the Recovery Vial and Daily Serum?

Recovery Vial (5mL): 0.1% GHK-Cu + centella + aloe. Minimal formula designed for disrupted skin - post-microneedling, post-peel, barrier damage. No niacinamide, no Matrixyl.

Daily Serum (15mL): 0.05% GHK-Cu + 3% Matrixyl 3000 + 3% niacinamide. More actives for everyday anti-aging on stable skin.

Think of Recovery as your emergency kit and Daily as your steady routine.

Can I use the Daily Serum immediately after microneedling?

No. Immediately post-needling, your skin has thousands of tiny channels open. Stick to the Recovery Vial (or your clinician's recommended serum) for the first 24-48 hours. Reintroduce Daily Serum only after your skin looks and feels calm.

What can I put on my skin right after microneedling?

Use the Recovery Vial or a gentle, fragrance-free, non-acidic hydrator. Avoid anything with exfoliating acids, vitamin C (at high concentrations), retinoids, or fragrance for at least 24-48 hours. When in doubt, ask your clinician.

Do I still need sunscreen if I'm using copper peptides?

Yes, always. Copper peptides don't provide any UV protection. Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily, especially if you're doing any treatments that disrupt your barrier.

How should I patch test LUMINA products?

Apply a small amount behind your ear or on your inner forearm. Wait 24-48 hours. If you see redness, itching, or irritation, don't use the product on your face. This is especially important if you have sensitive or reactive skin.

Can I use LUMINA if I don't microneedle at all?

Absolutely. The Daily Serum is designed for everyday anti-aging on stable skin - no procedures required. Keep a Recovery Vial on hand for occasional "overdid it" moments (post-peel, climate shock, barrier wobble), but you can use the Daily Serum as your main peptide product.

Are these products safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding?

We haven't conducted specific safety testing for pregnant or nursing individuals. If you're pregnant or breastfeeding, please consult your healthcare provider before using any new skincare products, including LUMINA.

What if my skin stings, burns, or stays red after using LUMINA?

Stop using the product immediately. Rinse gently with cool water and apply a bland, fragrance-free moisturizer. If irritation persists, consult a dermatologist. This is exactly what we mean by "listen to your skin" - if it pushes back, pull back.

How long until I see results?

Peptide-based skincare is a slow game. Most users report noticeable improvements in texture and firmness after 8-12 weeks of consistent use. If you're microneedling, you may see faster results, but the timeline varies by individual.
